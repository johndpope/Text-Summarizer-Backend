Canada's current account deficit widened  to a seasonally adjusted 2.27 billion dlrs in the fourth  quarter from a deficit of 1.94 billion dlrs in the third,  Statistics Canada said.
The agency said the rise in the merchandise surplus, to  2.77 billion dlrs from 2.20 billion dlrs in the third quarter,  was more than offset by high deficits in servicies and  investment.
In the capital account, not seasonally adjusted, there was  a total net inflow of 4.76 billion dlrs in the fourth quarter,  up from a net inflow of 3.22 billion dlrs in the third quarter  quarter.
