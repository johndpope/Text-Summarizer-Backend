Indonesia's state oil company, Pertamina,  has informed its affiliated Japanese companies that crude oil  supplies will be 40 pct less than contractual volumes in July,  a spokesman for an affiliated company said.
Allocations of Indonesia's main traded crude, Minas, had  been cut by 50 pct and Duri by 38 pct, he said.
A cargo of Duri loading in July was reportedly traded  yesterday at 70 cents over its osp of 15.60 dlrs per barrel and  Minas is trading about 20 cents higher, they said.
