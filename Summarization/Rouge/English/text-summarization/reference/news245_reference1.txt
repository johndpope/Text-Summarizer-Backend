Speaking after a meeting at the Bank for International  Settlements (BIS), which reviewed the agreement, they also  welcomed interest rates cuts in France and today's drop in  British rates.
One top official said the rate cuts would help  to stimulate growth generally in Europe and welcomed that  countries other than West Germany were seen to be helping  sustain the economy.
The central bankers, who spoke on the condition they not be  named, said the meeting of governors from the Group of 10  countries also heard a report from Michel Camdessus, the new  managing director of the International Monetary Fund, on the  IMF's latest assessment of the debt crisis.
