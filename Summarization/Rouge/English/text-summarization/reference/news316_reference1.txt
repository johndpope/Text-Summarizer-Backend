The Soviet oil production target for 1988  has been set at 625 mln tonnes, a rise of eight mln tonnes over  this year's planned output.
Oil Minister Vasily Dinkov announced the figure on Tuesday  at the Supreme Soviet, the country's nominal parliament.
Last year the Soviet Union produced 615 mln tonnes of oil,  slightly short of the target of 616.7 mln.
January-September figures released at the weekend showed  Soviet oil output in the first nine months of this year at 467  mln tonnes.
Dinkov said the Soviet Union would develop 38 new oil  deposits in the coming year.
