U.S. oil demand as measured by  products supplied rose 2.1 pct in the four weeks ended February  27 to 16.39 mln barrels per day (bpd) from 16.05 mln in the  same period a year ago, the Energy Information Administration  (EIA) said.
In its weekly petroleum status report, the Energy  Department agency said distillate demand was down 2.1 pct in  the period to 3.37 mln bpd from 3.44 mln a year earlier.
Domestic crude oil production was estimated at 8.38 mln  bpd, down 8.7 pct from 9.18 mln a year ago, and gross daily  crude imports (excluding those for the SPR) averaged 4.11 mln  bpd, up 36.9 pct from three mln, the EIA said.
