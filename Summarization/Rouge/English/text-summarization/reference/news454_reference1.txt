Inflation in the 24 industrialised  nations of the Organisation for Economic Cooperation and  Development was 2.1 pct in the year to January, unchanged from  December, the OECD said.
For OECD states the increase  was 0.7 pct but the January level of retail energy prices was  still 11.6 pct lower than a year earlier, the OECD said.
The increase in consumer prices excluding food and energy,  at 0.4 pct in January for the whole OECD area, was unchanged on  the four previous months, but over the year to January OECD  consumer prices rose by 3.5 pct, against 3.3 pct in December.
