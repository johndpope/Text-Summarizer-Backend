China has raised the prices it pays  farmers for cotton, edible oil, sugar cane and beets to reverse  a decline in output in 1986, He Kang, Minister of Agriculture,  Animal Husbandry and Fisheries said.
He  gave no details of the price increases.
On grain, He said the state will cut the quota it purchases  from farmers by 50 mln tonnes and abolish the practice of  purchasing through agents.
He said the state will increase investment in agriculture  and supplies of fertiliser, diesel oil and other production  materials and stabilise fertiliser and diesel oil prices.
