Indonesian sugar output is forecast by  the government to reach 2.126 mln tonnes in calendar 1987  against projected consumption of 2.039 mln tonnes, Agriculture  Minister Achmad Affandi said.
Production in 1986, when Indonesia was forced to import  sugar, is officially estimated at 2.016 mln tonnes against  1.728 mln in 1985.
Indonesia imported 162,000 tonnes of sugar towards the end  of last year and in early 1987 to boost low stocks.
Affandi told reporters stocks fel to as low as 159,000  tonnes at the beginning of 1987.
A spokesman for the National Logistics Bureau, which  distributes and stores sugar, said stocks this month were  489,437 tonnes.
