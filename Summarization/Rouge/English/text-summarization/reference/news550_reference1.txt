Copper shipments are likely to be  delayed because of power problems at &lt;Philippine Associated  Smelting and Refining Corp> (PASAR), the country's only  smelter, a company official said.
Asked to confirm reports by New York copper trade sources  about PASAR's shipment problems, marketing manager Deogracias  Madrid told Reuters, "They are partly correct.
The New York trade sources said if PASAR's shipments were  delayed, customers might have to turn to the London Metal  Exchange for supply.
A spokeswoman for the Chamber of Mines said Philippine  copper production amounted to 222,644 tonnes in 1986, down  slightly from 226,157 tonnes in 1985.
