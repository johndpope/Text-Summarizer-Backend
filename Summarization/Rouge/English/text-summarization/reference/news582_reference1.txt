Hong Kong's broadly defined M3 money  supply rose 2.2 pct to 607.17 billion H.K. Dlrs in January,  after a 3.1 pct rise in December, for a year-on-year rise of  23.3 pct, the government said in a statement.
Total M2 rose 3.3 pct to 535.26 billion dlrs in January  from December when it rose 3.5 pct on the previous month.
Local  M2 rose 4.7 pct to 249.03 billion dlrs in January from December  when it climbed 4.2 pct.
Total M1 rose 12 pct to 62.84 billion dlrs in January after  a 5.0 pct rise the previous month.
Loans for financing Hong Kong's visible trade rose 3.4 pct  to 36.72 billion dlrs after a 1.8 pct rise in December.
