The British Petroleum &lt;BP.L> Co Plc's  refinery at Grangemouth, Scotland, shut down after an explosion  and fire in the hydrocracker on March 22, will probably be back  in operation towards the week-end, a refinery spokesman said.
The explosion and fire, in which one worker was killed,  caused extensive damage to the central part of the 32,000  hydrocracker and the spokesman said today this unit would be  out of operation for several months.
In an earlier incident, two people were killed in an  explosion and fire at the refinery on March 13.
