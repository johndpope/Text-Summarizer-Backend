Henry Kaufman, managing director of  Salomon Brothers Inc, said the nomination of economist Alan  Greenspan to replace Federal Reserve Board Chairman Paul  Volcker is bearish in the near term for the fixed income and  currency markets.
The  fundamental direction of interest rates continues to be upward,  interrupted by intermittent rallies," Kaufman said in a  statement.
Kaufman said, "Unlike Paul Volcker, who entered the office  of the Federal Reserve chairman with strong credibility both  domestically and internationally, Mr Greenspan will have to  demonstrate both his competence and policy independence before  he can gain full confidence of the financial markets."
