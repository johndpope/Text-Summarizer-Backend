West German central bank money stock  was growing at an annualized 7.5 pct in February, unchanged  from the 7.5 pct reported in January, provisional data from the  Bundesbank showed.
The data showed that the stock grew at an annualized 8.6  pct over the six months to February, slower than the 9.4 pct  rise in the same period to January.
Of the two components comprising central bank money stock,  cash in circulation rose to 111.7 billion marks in February  from 110.9 billion in January, the Bundesbank data showed.
This yielded an annualized 8.8 pct rise over the six month  to February, up slightly from an 8.6 pct increase over the same  period to January.
