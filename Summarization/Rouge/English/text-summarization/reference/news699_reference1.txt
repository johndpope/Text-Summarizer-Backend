In a statement reacting to the five institutes' joint  spring report, Bangemann said that as far as the call for  bringing forward the 1990 tax reform was concerned --      "The government points out that the positive effects for  growth of its policy of consolidation (cutting the budget  deficit) must not be allowed to be endangered."      Bangemann also recalled that the scope of tax cuts planned  for 1988 had already been increased.
Three institutes predicted two pct economic growth in 1987,  with exports falling by 0.5 pct.
