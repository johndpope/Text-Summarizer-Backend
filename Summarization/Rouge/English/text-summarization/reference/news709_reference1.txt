Five regional oil producing nations  will gather in Caracas tommorrow for a two-day meeting expected  to center on ways to combat proposals for a U.S. tax on  imported petroleum, the Venezuela's ministry of energy and  mines said.
Oil ministers from Mexico, Trinidad and Tobago, Ecuador and  Venezuela will be on hand for the fifth meeting of the informal  group of Latin American and Caribbean Petroleum Exporters,  formed in 1983, it said.
Energy and Mines Minister Arturo Hernandez Grisanti said  the conference has no set agenda but one entire session Friday  will be devoted to proposals for a tax on imported oil.
