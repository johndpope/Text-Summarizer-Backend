The U.K. Longer leading cyclical  indicator rose in February by 5.8 pct after January's 1.9 pct  rise, figures from the Central Statistical Office show.
The indicator, base 1980, which shows trends in the economy  12 months ahead, was put at 108.7 in February compared with  102.7 in January and 99.5 in February 1986.
The shorter leading indicator, signalling trends six months  ahead, was put at 97.1 for January, the latest month for which  data were available, after December's 96.5.
The lagging index, which the CSO says shows a turning point  in the economy about a year after it happens, was at 92.4 in  January, down from 92.5 in December and 93.0 a year earlier.
