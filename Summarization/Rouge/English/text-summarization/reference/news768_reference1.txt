Taiwan's leading industrial organisation  said it will send its first buying mission to the U.S. Later  this year in an effort to reduce the country's trade surplus  with Washington.
A spokesman for the Chinese National Federation of  Industries told Reuters the mission was part of a broader plan  to switch large purchases to the U.S. From Japan.
Last year its members purchased about 4.5 billion  U.S. Dlrs worth of industrial products from Japan and about 1.8  billion from the U.S.
Taiwan's trade surplus with the U.S. Rose to a record 13.6  billion dlrs last year from 10.2 billion in 1985.
