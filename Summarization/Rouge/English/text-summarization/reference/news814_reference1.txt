Japanese corporate earnings will rise 8.9  pct in fiscal 1987/88 for the first year-on-year increase in  three years, partly because of the yen's stable exchange rate,  the Nomura research institute said.
Sales of all industries are predicted to rise 1.9 pct in  the year, which starts April 1, the research arm of Nomura  Securities Co said in a statement.
Recurrent profits were estimated to fall 20 pct in the  current fiscal year with sales forecast to drop 14 pct.
Recurrent profits of manufacturing industries are projected  to rise 29.6 pct next fiscal year against an estimated 40.1 pct  fall in the current year.
