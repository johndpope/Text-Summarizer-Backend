The Federal Home Loan Bank Board said  home mortgage rates fell from early February to early March to  their lowest point in nine years, but the rate of decline was  slower than it had been in recent months.
Rates for adjustable-rate mortgages decline eight basis  points from early February to 8.43 pct in early March, the bank  board said.
The average effective interest rate on all loans closed by  major mortgage lenders declined nine basis points from early  February to early March, the agency said.
For fixed-rate loans it was 9.36 pct, 14  basis points below a month earlier, the agency said.
