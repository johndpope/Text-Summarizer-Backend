Indonesia has protested to the European  Community (EC) about its plan to raise import duties on  vegetable oils, which will affect the country's palm oil  exports to the EC, Trade Minister Rachmat Saleh said.
"Indonesia, both individually and together with other  members of the Association of South-East Asian Nations (ASEAN),  has protested against the EC plan to increase duties on  vegetable oil imports," he told Indonesian reporters.
According to central bank figures, Indonesia exported  362,700 tonnes of crude palm oil to EC countries in calendar  1985 and 301,400 tonnes in the first 10 months of 1986.
