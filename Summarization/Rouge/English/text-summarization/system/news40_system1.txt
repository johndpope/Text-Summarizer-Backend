United Technologies Corp said  the decision by an international consortium not to develop a  new engine would have no impact on 1987 or 1988 earnings.
&lt;International Aero Engines>, IAE, 30 pct owned by United  Technologies' Pratt and Whitney division, has decided not to  launch a superfan version of its V2500 engine.
"We've told analysts that IAE's decision not to launch a  full development program of the IAE superfan for certification  in 1991 will have no short term impact on earnings," a United  Technologies spokesman told Reuters.
IAE's other owners are Rolls Royce PLC, &lt;Japanese Aero  Engines Corp>, Fiat SPA and &lt;MTU> of West Germany.
