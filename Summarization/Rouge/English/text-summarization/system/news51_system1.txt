Australia's broad money supply rose 10.3  pct in the year ended January, up from a revised 9.6 pct in  December, the Reserve Bank said.
Within the broad money total, non-bank financial  intermediaries rose by 0.2 pct from a revised decline of 0.2   in December and a previous January's 0.8 pct increase.
In the January year, NBFI's borrowings rose by 9.5 pct from  a revised 10.1 in December and compared with a previous  January's 12.8.
